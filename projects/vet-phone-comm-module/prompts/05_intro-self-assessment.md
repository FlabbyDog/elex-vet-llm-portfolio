3. Introduction
- Purpose: Provide a high-level overview of the section and explain its importance.
- Components to Use: Key Topics
- Description: The broad subjects or themes that the instructional resource will cover.
- Purpose: To provide a high-level overview of the main areas of focus.
- Relation: Key topics are overarching themes under which more specific subtopics and sections are organized.
- Instructions:
1. Identify the Key Topics relevant to the section.
2. Write a brief overview that explains the importance of these key topics.
3. Connect the key topics to the overall context of the instructional resource.

4. Self-Assessment
- Purpose: Help learners evaluate their current understanding of the section.
- Components to Use: Supporting Questions
- Description: Questions designed to guide exploration and understanding of the content.
- Purpose: To prompt critical thinking, reflection, and deeper engagement with the material.
- Relation: Supporting questions are aligned with supporting points and goals, helping to clarify and reinforce key concepts.
- Instructions:
1. Develop self-assessment questions based on the Supporting Questions component.
2. Ensure the questions are open-ended and prompt learners to reflect on their understanding.
3. Include a mix of questions that assess both factual knowledge and deeper comprehension.
4. Include questions that prompt reflection on client interactions and team collaboration.

5. Skills Gap Analysis
- Purpose: Analyze the skills required to understand and apply the information in this section, and identify any gaps in learners' knowledge or abilities.
- Components to Use: Subtopics, Sections
- Subtopics:
- Description: Specific areas of focus within a key topic that further break down the main theme into manageable segments.
- Purpose: To narrow down the key topic into detailed aspects for in-depth exploration.
- Relation: Subtopics fall under key topics and encompass various sections that address specific components or aspects of the subtopic.
- Sections:
- Description: Divisions within a subtopic that cover particular aspects, skills, or concepts in detail.
- Purpose: To provide structured, focused content that addresses specific elements of a subtopic.
- Relation: Sections are the building blocks of subtopics, organizing content into logical, manageable parts.
- Instructions:
a. Identify the Subtopics and Sections relevant to the skills being analyzed.
b. Describe the skills required for each subtopic and section.
c. Assess learners' current proficiency in these skills.
d. Identify any gaps in knowledge or abilities.
e. Plan for developing the necessary skills, which could include additional resources, practice activities, or targeted instruction.
f. Consider skills related to managing client experiences and improving team interactions.
